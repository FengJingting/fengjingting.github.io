Research and Projects

Decision Planning for Automatic Robots in RoboMaster

• Simulated the real scenario in RoboMaster competition by Gazebo and Rviz and created raster map to help the robot locate itself.

• Realize decision planning of automatic robot through Ros2 and behaviour tree library, which includes navigation, patrol, escape, shooting and some other behaviors.

• Simulated the motion of robots and facilities in real competition by Unity and trained the navigation and obstacle avoidance ability of agents by reinforcement learning (PPO).

Difficulties: Because in the real competition, some robots are controlled by operators and others are automatic, it is difficult to let the automatic one collaborate with the human-controlled one without message sharing. What’s more, simulate the behaviour of human-controlled robots to train the robot is also difficult to achieve.

Reinforcement Learning in MOBA Games

Advisor: Prof. Xing Huanlai Funded Corporation: Tencent

• Researched on PPO, actor-critic network and other basic reinforcement learning models.

• Learned the general framework of game AI system and decision-making strategies in both 1V1 game and multi-agent game.

• Optimized the reinforcement learning model through adding noise network, adjusting relevant parameters depending on the performance of AI and so on.

• Rank top 10 in the 3rd AI Arena Multi-agent Reinforcement Learning Competition.

Innovations: By observing the performance of game AI, I discovered that in some cases, the model will be trapped in local optimal choices, which was adverse for battles against enemies. Thus, I added noise networks in the action spaces to improve the models behaviour.

Trajectory Prediction of Autonomous Cars Based on Transformer Model

Advisor: Dr.Li Ke

• Predicted motions of autonomous driving cars through spatial and temporal features of different agents in real world traffic, including pedestrians, bicycles, vehicles. And using spatial and temporal transformer model to generate results and the framework was realized by Pytorch .

• We found that the previous model was too simple for real world traffic situation. Thus, we combined the traffic conditions like roads and traffic with the target agents to optimize the model’s prediction results.

Innovation: Because our work was based on the work of other specialists, we found that during the process of generating trajectories, they discarded some seemingly underperforming trajectories which may be useful for prediction results. Thus, we added them back and weighed them together. By doing this, we improved the accuracy of the prediction results.
